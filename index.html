Paul Leask

Located in Leeds, UK

Hello! My name is Paul, and I am a mathematical physicist specialising in the areas of topological solitons, nuclear & high energy physics, and cosmology. I am currently a final year P.hD. student at the University of Leeds (under the supervision of Derek Harland and Martin Speight) and a member of the Leeds Geometry Group.

My main research project focuses on the quantization of skyrmion crystals stabilized by vector mesons (\(\omega\) and \(\rho\)). I am particularly intesrested in the application of these Skyrme model variants to cold dense nuclear matter (neutron stars, strange stars & black holes), and finite atomic nuclei.

If you are interested in my research, most of my recent and past research projects are detailed here. Within the Skyrme model, there are a few open problems that I am trying to address. In no particular order, these are:

  • Compression modulus: The compression modulus of neutron stars within the generalized Skyrme model is too large, \(K_0 \approx E_0\). The inclusion of the \(\omega\)-meson like sextic term is essential for the descritption of the high density regime within neutron stars (increasing the maximal mass from \(1.7M_\odot\) [Nelmes & Piette, 2011] to above \(2M_\odot\) [Adam et al., 2020]). The caveat of including the sextic term is it stiffens the equation of state, thereby increasing the compression modulus. \(\rho\)-mesons have the opposite effect, they soften the equation of state! Can the inclusion of vector mesons resolve this compression modulus issue while still predicting sufficiently large neutron stars?
  • Hyperon puzzle: The presence of kaons or hyperons leads to the strong softening of the equation of state of dense matter, resulting in maximal neutron star masses incompatible with recent observations \(\sim 2M_\odot\). It is known that strangeness can be incorporated into the Skyrme model by describing hyperons as bound kaon-skyrmion states [Callan & Klebanov, 1985]. This approach allows for the appearance of charged kaon condensates in neutron star cores, whilst still predicting maximal masses in the range \(1.5 \sim 2.5 M_\odot\) [Adam et al., 2023]. However, in addition to kaons, one should really include hyperon degrees of freedom and quantize the full \(SU(N_f=3)\) rotation group.
  • Binding energies: A long standing problem in the Skyrme model is the prediction of too large binding energies in the Bethe-Weizsäcker SEMF. There have been a few attempts at this problem, such as the inclusion of \(\rho\)-mesons [Naya & Sutcliffe, 2018] or the lightly bound model [Gillard, Harland & Speight, 2015]. However, heavier mesons also need to be included and how can one even estimate the SEMF coefficients using the Skyrme model?
